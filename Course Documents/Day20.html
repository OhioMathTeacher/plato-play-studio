Today's Goals: Set up staging and do final blocking, perform the allegory of the cave (~15 min), and record the performance. See Problem Set 4 for full guidelines.
🎭 PERFORMANCE DAY! — The culmination of two weeks of collaborative work. Celebrate the process and the product!
1. Logistics & Staging (25-30 minutes: 8:30-9:00)

Cave Setup (10 min)

  • Production Crew (Pair D) leads the staging
  • Create the "cave" space:
    • Arrange chairs/desks to define the space
    • Dim lights if possible (or designate "dark" area)
    • Set up any shadow effects (flashlight on wall?)
  • Create "outside" space:
    • Brighter area (near windows?)
    • Where does the freed prisoner emerge?
  • Camera placement: Where can we capture the whole scene?

Blocking Walk-Through (15-20 min)

  • Walk through each scene WITHOUT full dialogue
  • Scene 1:
    • Where do prisoners sit/stand?
    • How is one freed? Where do they go?
    • Exit point for transition to Scene 2?
  • Scene 2:
    • How do we show the "painful ascent"?
    • Where is the "sun" / bright light?
    • Physical journey across the space?
  • Scene 3:
    • Return to cave—how does that look?
    • Where are the other prisoners?
    • How does it end?
  • Narrator: Where do they stand? Separate from action?

Tech Check (5 min)

  • Have the final PDF script from Plato Play Draft Studio available as your master reference
  • Test camera angle—can we see everything?
  • Audio check—can we hear everyone?
  • One quick run-through of opening if time permits
2. Performance & Recording (15-20 minutes: 9:00-9:20)
Before you start: This is the performance. Let's give it our full energy. If something goes wrong, keep going—that's theater! We can do a second take if needed.

The Performance (~15 min)

  • Start recording!
  • Performance order:
    1. Narrator: Opening (~2 min)
    2. Scene 1: The Cave + Liberation (~5 min)
    3. Narrator: Transition (~1 min)
    4. Scene 2: The Ascent (~5 min)
    5. Narrator: Transition (~1 min)
    6. Scene 3: The Return (~5 min)
    7. Narrator: Closing (~2 min)
  • Let it flow—don't stop unless absolutely necessary
  • Stop recording after narrator's closing

Second Take? (if needed)

  • Quick debrief: Was there a major issue we should fix?
  • If yes: Do another take of the problematic section
  • If no: One take is fine! Imperfection is authentic.
3. Reactions & Celebration (15 minutes: 9:20-9:35)

Immediate Reactions (10 min)

  • How did that FEEL?
  • Go around the room—one word or sentence from each person
  • What moment worked better than you expected?
  • What was harder than you thought it would be?

Celebration (5 min)

  • Acknowledge the collaborative achievement
  • You just did something almost no one does—turned Plato's Allegory into a live performance.
  • This was a whole-class creation. Everyone contributed.
  • Applause for the ensemble!
