Today's Goals: Groups complete drafts of their scenes (with stage directions), full table read—everyone hears the whole play together for the first time, and identify what's working and what needs revision
1. Complete Drafts (25 minutes: 8:30-9:00)

Check-In (5 min)

  • Where did each group land? How far along are your drafts?
  • Quick pulse check—anyone stuck? Need help?
  • Goal for the next 25 minutes: Have a READABLE draft. Doesn't have to be perfect.

Group Work Time (20 min)

  • Scene Groups (A, B, C):
    • Finalize dialogue
    • Add stage directions (entrances, exits, movements, emotions)
    • Make sure your scene connects to the scenes before/after it
  • Production Crew (D):
    • Write narrator script: intro, transitions between scenes, closing
    • Sketch staging plan: Where is the "cave"? Where is "outside"?
    • List any props/materials needed (keep it simple!)
2. Table Read (30 minutes: 9:00-9:30)

Setup (2 min)

  • Everyone sits in a circle or around tables
  • Scripts in hand (printed or on screens)
  • We're going to read through the entire play, start to finish.
  • As you listen, mark anything that:
    • Doesn't land (confusing, awkward)
    • Needs more (missing something)
    • Works well (note it!)

Read Through (~20 min)

  • Order:
    1. Narrator: Opening (Pair D)
    2. Scene 1: The Cave + Liberation (Triad A)
    3. Narrator: Transition (Pair D)
    4. Scene 2: The Ascent (Triad B)
    5. Narrator: Transition (Pair D)
    6. Scene 3: The Return (Triad C)
    7. Narrator: Closing (Pair D)
  • Groups read their own scenes (assign roles within group)
  • Read stage directions aloud too (helps everyone picture it)
  • Don't stop to fix things—just read through and note issues

Feedback Discussion (8 min)

  • How did that feel? What's working?
  • What moments were confusing or didn't land?
  • Where do transitions feel rough?
  • Collect specific, actionable feedback
3. Revision Time (20 minutes: 9:30-9:50)

Back to Groups (15 min)

  • Based on what you heard, revise your section.
  • Scene Groups:
    • Punch up dialogue that fell flat
    • Clarify stage directions
    • Strengthen your ending
  • Production Crew:
    • Refine narrator script and transitions
    • Finalize staging plan
    • Coordinate with scene groups on transitions

Share Revised Sections (5 min)

  • Groups update the shared doc with revisions
  • Quick check: Everyone has access to the full script?
Before Thursday (Day 20):
  • Know your part — doesn't need to be memorized, but be comfortable
  • Simple props/costumes: If you want to bring something, keep it simple
    • Robe = draped fabric
    • Chains = scarves or rope
    • Nothing required—but can add to the experience
  • Production crew: Finalize cave staging plan. What do you need?

Come ready to perform! We'll do staging/blocking, then record.
