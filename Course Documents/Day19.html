Today's Goals: Groups complete drafts of their scenes (with stage directions), use Plato Play Draft Studio to assemble the full combined script, full table read—everyone hears the whole play together for the first time, and identify what's working and what needs revision
1. Complete Drafts (25 minutes: 8:30-9:00)

Check-In (5 min)

  • Where did each group land? How far along are your drafts?
  • Quick pulse check—anyone stuck? Need help?
  • Goal for the next 25 minutes: Have a READABLE draft. Doesn't have to be perfect.
  • Teacher: open Plato Play Draft Studio on the projector. Use Pull Doc for each group to show live progress on the big screen—groups can see immediately whether their markup is rendering correctly.

Group Work Time (20 min)

  • Scene Groups (A, B, C):
    • Finalize dialogue
    • Add stage directions (entrances, exits, movements, emotions)
    • Make sure your scene connects to the scenes before/after it
  • Production Crew (D):
    • Write narrator script: intro, transitions between scenes, closing
    • Sketch staging plan: Where is the "cave"? Where is "outside"?
    • List any props/materials needed (keep it simple!)
2. Table Read (30 minutes: 9:00-9:30)

Assemble the Combined Script in Plato Play Draft Studio (3 min)

  • Teacher: in the studio, click View Combined—this pulls all four Google Docs at once and merges them into a single formatted script (Pair D cue markers are inserted automatically between scenes)
  • Review the combined preview for any obvious formatting gaps; groups fix their markup if needed
  • Click PDF to open a print-friendly tab. Print or save as PDF and distribute—everyone now has the full script in hand (printed or on screen)

Setup (2 min)

  • Everyone sits in a circle or around tables, combined script in hand
  • The combined script is also displayed in the studio on the projector for reference
  • We're going to read through the entire play, start to finish.
  • As you listen, mark anything that:
    • Doesn't land (confusing, awkward)
    • Needs more (missing something)
    • Works well (note it!)

Read Through (~20 min)

  • Order:
    1. Narrator: Opening (Pair D)
    2. Scene 1: The Cave + Liberation (Triad A)
    3. Narrator: Transition (Pair D)
    4. Scene 2: The Ascent (Triad B)
    5. Narrator: Transition (Pair D)
    6. Scene 3: The Return (Triad C)
    7. Narrator: Closing (Pair D)
  • Groups read their own scenes (assign roles within group)
  • Read stage directions aloud too (helps everyone picture it)
  • Don't stop to fix things—just read through and note issues

Feedback Discussion (8 min)

  • How did that feel? What's working?
  • What moments were confusing or didn't land?
  • Where do transitions feel rough?
  • Collect specific, actionable feedback
3. Revision Time (20 minutes: 9:30-9:50)

Back to Groups (15 min)

  • Based on what you heard, revise your section.
  • Optional: Have Close Reader open on your device so you can reference Plato's exact language as you revise. You might want to strengthen a line by anchoring it more closely to the text, or catch a detail you missed.
  • Scene Groups:
    • Punch up dialogue that fell flat
    • Clarify stage directions
    • Strengthen your ending (try anchoring language closer to Plato's text if it helps)
  • Production Crew:
    • Refine narrator script and transitions (Close Reader can help you pull exact language if needed)
    • Finalize staging plan
    • Coordinate with scene groups on transitions

Share Revised Sections (5 min)

  • Groups save their revisions in their Google Doc
  • Teacher: click View Combined again in the studio to re-pull all docs and confirm the final combined script looks correct in the formatted preview
  • Use Export Project to save a JSON backup of the assembled script; use PDF to generate a clean final copy for Day 20
Before Thursday (Day 20):
  • Know your part — doesn't need to be memorized, but be comfortable
  • Simple props/costumes: If you want to bring something, keep it simple
    • Robe = draped fabric
    • Chains = scarves or rope
    • Nothing required—but can add to the experience
  • Production crew: Finalize cave staging plan. What do you need?

Come ready to perform! We'll do staging/blocking, then record.
